# Discount Mechanisms — Reference Index

> Context: B2C SaaS/App targeting Southeast Asia, Europe, and Global markets.
> This document is a living index. Each mechanism links to its own deep-dive file.

---

## What Is a Discount Mechanism?

A discount mechanism is any structured technique that reduces the effective price a customer pays — either temporarily, conditionally, or permanently — in order to achieve a business objective. Discount mechanisms are distinct from revenue models: a revenue model defines *how* a product charges; a discount mechanism modifies *how much* it charges under specific conditions.

Used well, discount mechanisms drive acquisition, improve retention, accelerate upgrade decisions, and recover churned users. Used poorly, they erode margins, train price-sensitive behaviour, and anchor users to a price they are unwilling to leave.

## Mechanism Overview

| Mechanism | Primary Objective | Revenue Impact | Best Market Fit | Churn Risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Introductory Pricing | Acquisition | Medium | All | Low (if scoped) |
| Flash Sales | Demand spike / Reactivation | Short-term high | Global, SEA | Medium |
| Seasonal Promotions | Demand spike | Predictable spike | Global, EU | Low |
| Volume Discounts | Higher commitment / ARPU | Medium | Credit/token models | Low |
| Bundle Discounts | ARPU expansion / Cross-sell | Medium–High | Subscription + add-ons | Low |
| Loyalty Rewards | Retention / LTV | Long-term high | High-frequency products | Very low |
| Referral Discounts | Acquisition (viral) | Low CAC | Social products | Low |
| Tenure Discounts | Retention / Churn prevention | Medium | Subscription | Very low |
| First-Purchase Discount | Acquisition / Conversion | Short-term low | E-commerce, one-time | Low |
| Promo Codes | Channel-specific acquisition | Variable | All | Low |
| Annual Prepay Discount | Cash flow / Churn reduction | High (upfront) | Subscription | Very low |
| Win-Back Offers | Re-acquisition (churned users) | Marginal recovery | Subscription | Medium |

## Mechanism Summaries

### Time-Based Discounts

#### 1. [Introductory Pricing](./discounts/01-introductory-pricing.md)
A reduced price offered at launch or to new users for a defined period or cohort. Creates an early-adopter incentive and lowers the initial conversion barrier. Requires a clear sunset mechanism and should never be extended indefinitely.

#### 2. [Flash Sales](./discounts/02-flash-sales.md)
Short-duration (24–72 hr), deep discounts used to create urgency and drive acquisition or reactivation spikes. Effective for time-sensitive campaigns but risks training discount-seeking behaviour if used too frequently.

#### 3. [Seasonal Promotions](./discounts/03-seasonal-promotions.md)
Discounts tied to calendar events: Black Friday, New Year, back-to-school, regional holidays. Predictable demand spikes that users increasingly expect. Most effective when scoped to specific cohorts (new users only) to avoid gifting discounts to users who would have converted anyway.

---

### Value-Based Discounts

#### 4. [Volume Discounts](./discounts/04-volume-discounts.md)
Reduced per-unit price at higher purchase quantities — most commonly applied to credit packs, token bundles, or seat counts. Encourages larger upfront commitment and higher ARPU per transaction. Particularly relevant for usage-based and credit-model B2C products.

#### 5. [Bundle Discounts](./discounts/05-bundle-discounts.md)
Multiple products, features, or services packaged together at a lower combined price than purchasing individually. Increases average order value, drives cross-sell, and increases perceived value. A cornerstone mechanic for hybrid revenue models.

---

### Loyalty & Relationship Discounts

#### 6. [Loyalty Rewards](./discounts/06-loyalty-rewards.md)
Points, credits, or cashback earned through ongoing product use, redeemable against future purchases or upgrades. Builds strong retention mechanics and increases switching costs. Most effective in high-frequency use products.

#### 7. [Referral Discounts](./discounts/07-referral-discounts.md)
A discount (or credit) granted to both the referrer and the newly acquired user. One of the most capital-efficient customer acquisition mechanics when LTV justifies it. Creates viral acquisition loops and is particularly powerful in social or collaborative B2C products.

#### 8. [Tenure Discounts](./discounts/08-tenure-discounts.md)
Reduced pricing or added value (bonus credits, feature unlocks) offered to long-standing subscribers as a reward for loyalty or as a proactive churn-prevention measure. Rarely disclosed upfront; most commonly deployed as a retention lever at the cancellation moment.

---

### Acquisition Discounts

#### 9. [First-Purchase Discount](./discounts/09-first-purchase-discount.md)
A one-time reduction applied to a new user's first transaction. Lowers the psychological risk of the first payment decision. Common in e-commerce and one-time purchase models; applies to SaaS as a first-month reduction.

#### 10. [Promo Codes](./discounts/10-promo-codes.md)
Unique codes distributed via marketing channels, influencers, partnerships, or campaigns, redeemable at checkout for a specific discount. High flexibility for targeted campaigns with measurable attribution. Risks include code leakage and margin erosion if codes are stacked or distributed too broadly.

---

### Retention & Recovery Discounts

#### 11. [Annual Prepay Discount](./discounts/11-annual-prepay-discount.md)
A meaningful reduction (typically 15–25%) offered to users who commit to annual billing upfront vs. monthly. Simultaneously improves cash flow, reduces churn probability, and increases LTV. The highest ROI discount mechanism for subscription B2C products.

#### 12. [Win-Back Offers](./discounts/12-win-back-offers.md)
Targeted discounts sent to churned or lapsed users to re-engage them. Typically delivered via email after a defined dormancy period. Effective for recovering users who churned for price reasons; less effective for users who churned due to product or fit issues.

## Strategic Considerations

### Discount discipline
Every discount is a permanent signal about your product's actual price floor. Run too many overlapping discounts and users will wait for the next sale rather than paying full price. A disciplined discount strategy means each mechanism has a defined purpose, scope, target cohort, and expiry.

### Price anchoring
Introductory pricing, first-purchase discounts, and flash sales all create anchors. Before deploying them, be clear on the upgrade path: can you credibly walk users from the discounted price to full price, and why will they accept that?

### Market sensitivity
- **SEA:** Price sensitivity is high; discounts have outsized conversion impact. But discount-trained cohorts in SEA also show lower LTV. Annual prepay discounts and referral mechanics (especially e-wallet cashback framing) tend to outperform flash sales in LTV terms.
- **Europe:** Seasonal promotions and annual prepay discounts perform well. GDPR and EU consumer law require clear disclosure of the pre-discount "reference price" (EU Omnibus Directive) — showing a crossed-out price requires that price to have been the actual price for at least 30 days prior.
- **Global:** Promo codes and referral discounts scale well globally with minimal localisation overhead.

### Interaction with revenue models
Discount mechanisms are not revenue models — they are modifiers applied to the base revenue model. See each deep-dive for a detailed mapping of which revenue models each mechanism pairs with and which combinations create conflicts.
